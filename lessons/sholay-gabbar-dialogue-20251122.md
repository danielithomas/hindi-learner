# Lesson: The Famous Gabbar Dialogue from Sholay

**Date:** 22 November 2025  
**Topic:** Iconic Bollywood dialogue analysis  
**Level:** Intermediate-Beginner

## The Dialogue

```
Gabbar: Kitne aadmi the?
Kaliya: Do, sarkar.
Gabbar: Do aadmi... aur tum teen? Phir bhi laut aaye khaali haath?
```

## Full Translation

**Gabbar:** How many men were there?  
**Kaliya:** Two, sir.  
**Gabbar:** Two men... and you three? Still, you returned empty-handed?

## Key Grammar Concepts

### 1. Compound Verbs
**‡§≤‡•å‡§ü ‡§Ü‡§Ø‡•á** (laut aaye) - "returned"
- This is a compound verb combining:
  - ‡§≤‡•å‡§ü (laut) - to return/turn back
  - ‡§Ü‡§®‡§æ (aana) - to come
- Together they emphasise the completion of returning

### 2. Conjunctions
**‡§´‡§ø‡§∞ ‡§≠‡•Ä** (phir bhi) - "still/nevertheless/even then"
- This shows contrast or surprise
- Used when something unexpected happens despite circumstances

**‡§î‡§∞** (aur) - "and"
- Simple conjunction to connect words or clauses

### 3. Idioms and Expressions
**‡§ñ‡§æ‡§≤‡•Ä ‡§π‡§æ‡§•** (khaali haath) - "empty-handed"
- Literally: "empty hands"
- Used when someone returns without success or without what they went to get
- Common in everyday conversation

## Cultural Context: Why This Dialogue is Famous

This dialogue from the 1975 film *Sholay* has become legendary in Indian cinema because:

1. **Iconic villain:** Gabbar Singh is one of Bollywood's most memorable villains
2. **Powerful delivery:** The way the lines are spoken conveys menace and authority
3. **Everyday language:** Uses simple, conversational Hindi that everyone understands
4. **Memorable scene:** The interrogation scene is tense and dramatic
5. **Cultural impact:** Indians of all ages can quote these lines

The dialogue shows a leader (Gabbar) expressing disbelief and anger that his three men failed against only two opponents.
